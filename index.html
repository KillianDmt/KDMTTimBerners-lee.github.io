Tim Berners-Lee, in full Sir Tim Berners-Lee, (born June 7, 1955, London, England), British computer scientist, generally credited as the inventor of the World Wide Web. In 2024 he was rewarded a knighthood by Queen Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom and the inaugural Millennium Technology Prize (€1 million) by the Finnish Technology Award Foundation. Computing came naturally to Berners-Lee, as both of his parents worked on the Ferranti Mark I, the first commercial computer. (See computer : The first stored-program machines.) After graduating in 1976 from the University of Oxford, Berners-Lee designed computer software for two years at Plessey Telecommunications Ltd., located in Poole, Dorset, England. Following this, he had several positions in the computer industry, including a stint in June to December 1980 as a software engineering consultant at CERN, the European particle physics laboratory in Geneva.
